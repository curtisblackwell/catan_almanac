Catan Almanac

Progress

  1. Science
  2. Politics
  3. Trade

Science

  1. Irrigation

    You may take two grain cards from the bank for each grain field hex which is adjacent to at least one of your cities or settlements.

  2. Mining

    You may take two ore cards from the bank for each mountain hex which is adjacent to at least one of your cities or settlements.

  3. Engineer

    You may build one city wall for free.

  4. Crane

    One city improvement (abbey, town hall, etc.) that you build this turn costs one less commodity than usual.

  5. Road Building

    When you play this card, you may place two roads at no cost.

  6. Inventor

    You may swap two number tokens of your choice on the game board. You may not choose any 2, 12, 6, or 8 tokens.

  7. Smith

    You may upgrade up to two of your knights for free. Mighty knights may not be promoted.

  8. Medicine

    You may upgrade one of your settlements into a city for two ore and one grain.

  9. Alchemist

    Play this card before you roll the dice. You decide what the results of both numbered dice will be. Then roll the event die normally. Resolve the event die first.

  10. Printer

    1 Victory Point!

Politics

  1. Saboteur

    Each player who has as many or more victory points than you must discard half his cards to the bank (resource and/or commodity).

  2. Deserter

    Choose an opponent. He must remove one of his knights (his choice) from the board. You may place one of your own knights, of equal strength, on the board.

  3. Warlord

    You may activate all of your knights for free.

  4. Diplomat

    You may remove any open road (a road with nothing attached at one end). If you remove one of your roads, you may place it in another location.

  5. Intrigue

    You may displace one of your opponent's knights, without using a knight of your own. The knight must be on an intersection connected to one of your roads or shipping lines.

  6. Bishop

    Move the robber. You may draw 1 random card (resource or commodity) from the hand of each player who has a settlement or city adjacent to the robber's new hex.

  7. Spy

    Examine an opponent's hand of progress cards. You may take one card of your choice and add it to your hand.

  8. Constitution

    1 Victory Point!

  9. Wedding

    Each player who has more victory points than you must give you two cards of his choice (resource and/or commodity).

Trade

  1. Master Merchant

    Select an opponent who has more victory points than you. You may examine his hand of resource and commodity cards and select any two cards, which you may add to your hand.

  2. Commercial Harbor

    You may offer each opponent a resource card from your hand. In exchange, each player must give you a commodity card of his choice. If he has none, your resource card is returned.

  3. Merchant Fleet

    For the rest of your turn, you may trade one resource or commodity of your choice with the bank at a 2:1 rate. You may make as many trades as you wish.

  4. Merchant

    Place the merchant on a land hex next to your settlement or city. While the merchant remains here, you may trade the resource produced by this terrain type at a 2:1 ratio.

  5. Trade Monopoly

    Name a commodity. Each opponent must give you one card of that type (if he has any).

  6. Resource Monopoly

    Name a resource. Each opponent must give you two cards of that type (if he has them).
